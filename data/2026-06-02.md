### [1] [SPARQLe: Sub-Precision Activation Representation for Quantized LLM Inference](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.00365)
*Aradhana Mohan Parvathy,Soumendu Kumar Ghosh,Shamik Kundu,Arnab Raha,Souvik Kundu,Deepak A. Mathaikutty,Anand Raghunathan*

Main category: cs.AR

TL;DR: SPARQLe是一个硬件-软件协同设计框架，利用激活值在零附近集中分布的统计特性，将2k位激活张量分解为密集的k位LSB张量和用精度位图压缩的稀疏k位MSB张量，在保持精度的同时减少内存流量并实现高效计算，在BitNet 3B、Llama2 7B和Llama3 8B上取得16-24.3%的预填充延迟降低和13.5-23.4%的解码延迟降低。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 大语言模型规模快速增长导致推理时计算和内存成本高昂。量化是解决该问题的关键路径，但激活值往往需要比权值更高的精度（如8位 vs 4位），迫使硬件使用高精度数据通路，效率低下。

Method: SPARQLe利用激活值大量集中在零附近的统计特性（高阶位存在稀疏性）。将每个2k位激活张量表示为密集的k位LSB张量和一个用精度位图压缩的稀疏k位MSB张量，并提出轻量级算法增加MSB稀疏性。配套加速器直接操作该混合格式，控制开销极小。

Result: 在BitNet 3B、Llama2 7B和Llama3 8B上，预填充延迟降低16-24.3%，解码延迟降低13.5-23.4%；预填充能量降低17-26.7%，解码能量降低6.5-14.2%。

Conclusion: 子精度激活稀疏性为高效LLM推理提供了一条有效且互补的路径。

Abstract: The rapid growth in sizes of Large language models (LLMs) results in high compute and memory costs during inference. Quantization has been a significant pathway to addressing this challenge. In the quest to push the limits of quantization, weights, which are static, can often be quantized aggressively (e.g. 4 bits), while activations often require higher precision (e.g., 8 bits) to preserve accuracy, forcing hardware to operate with higher-precision datapaths. We leverage the statistical property that a significant fraction of activations are concentrated around zero, resulting in sparsity in the higher-order bits. Our proposal, SPARQLe, is a hardware-software co-design framework that exploits this sub-precision redundancy in any given quantized model. SPARQLe represents each 2k-bit activation tensor as a dense k-bit LSB tensor and a sparse k-bit MSB tensor compressed with a precision bitmap, and proposes a lightweight algorithm to increase MSB sparsity. SPARQLe reduces activation memory traffic and enables efficient computation on k-bit datapaths while preserving 2k-bit activation accuracy. SPARQLe includes an accelerator that operates directly on this hybrid format with minimal control overheads. Across the BitNet 3B, Llama2 7B, and Llama3 8B models, SPARQLe reduces prefill latency by 16-24.3% and decode latency by 13.5-23.4%, with 17-26.7% and 6.5-14.2% lower prefill and decode energy, respectively. SPARQLe demonstrates that sub-precision activation sparsity offers an effective and complementary pathway towards efficient LLM inference.

### [2] [Regular-Dead on Arrival: Characterizing and Protecting Against Dead-Entry TLB Misses in GPU Microarchitectures](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.00486)
*Shafayat Mowla Anik,Yongchan Jung,Jeeho Ryoo,Byeong Kil Lee*

Main category: cs.AR

TL;DR: 本文针对GPU工作负载中L2 TLB的冗余缺失问题，提出了一种基于布隆过滤器的保护机制DEPOT，可显著提升IPC性能。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: GPU大内存工作负载中频繁出现冗余L2 TLB缺失（死条目缺失），即刚被驱逐的翻译条目立即需要重新页遍历，造成严重性能损失。

Method: 提出两分类法：突发放大（warp共享同一虚拟页）和容量溢出（每个warp访问不同页集）。基于此设计DEPOT，使用1 KB布隆过滤器保护最近驱逐的翻译条目不被立即替换。

Result: 在干扰驱动型工作负载上IPC提升高达72%，其他负载零开销；与现有TLB预取和压缩机制结合可额外获得2-7%的性能提升。

Conclusion: DEPOT能有效消除死条目缺失，且与现有技术正交互补，是一种轻量高效的TLB优化方案。

Abstract: GPU workloads with large memory footprints frequently suffer from redundant L2 TLB misses in which a recently evicted translation is immediately re-walked at full page-walk cost. We characterize these dead-entry misses across 24 GPU workloads, finding they account for up to 99% of L2 TLB misses in the most TLB-sensitive applications, yet their performance impact varies widely depending on memory access structure. Workloads where warps share the same virtual page suffer from burst amplification, where a single eviction stalls many warps simultaneously waiting for one translation to return. In contrast, workloads where each warp accesses a distinct set of pages face a capacity-overflow problem that no replacement policy can resolve, a distinction validated by huge page experiments. Building on this two-class taxonomy, we design DEPOT (Dead-Entry PrOTection), a 1 KB Bloom filter mechanism that prevents recently evicted translations from being displaced immediately upon reinstallation, delivering up to 72% IPC improvement on interference-driven workloads with zero overhead on others, and composing with the state-of-the-art TLB prefetching and compaction mechanism, for 2 to 7% additional gain.

A full accuracy sweep across five thresholds reveals that UNet+transformer workloads degrade gracefully, while motion models exhibit an accuracy cliff between the primary operating point and the next threshold. Our characterization shows that workload group and model dimensions jointly determine whether column-level memory layout optimization is beneficial, and element-level sparsity alone is insufficient for that prediction.
