### [1] [Energy Efficient Software Hardware CoDesign for Machine Learning: From TinyML to Large Language Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.23668)
*Mohammad Saleh Vahdatpour,Yanqing Zhang*

Main category: cs.AR

TL;DR: 这篇论文综述了从边缘设备到数据中心LLM服务的能效软硬件协同设计方法，分析了数据移动和内存系统对性能能耗的限制，提出了分层分解视角来指导构建能源和碳感知的ML系统。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 随着机器学习从毫瓦级TinyML设备到大型语言模型的广泛部署，能源效率已成为可持续AI的主要约束。在不同规模上，性能和能耗越来越受数据移动和内存系统行为的限制，而非仅受算术吞吐量限制。

Method: 论文回顾了能效软硬件协同设计方法，涵盖边缘推理和训练到数据中心规模的LLM服务，包括加速器架构（ASIC/FPGA数据流、处理/计算内存设计）和系统级技术（分区、量化、调度、运行时适应）。

Result: 提炼了常见的设计杠杆和权衡，突出了重复出现的差距：跨平台泛化有限、协同设计搜索空间大且成本高、跨工作负载和部署设置的基准测试不一致。

Conclusion: 提出了分层分解视角，将优化策略映射到计算角色并支持增量适应，为构建能源和碳感知的ML系统提供实用指导。

Abstract: The rapid deployment of machine learning across platforms from milliwatt-class TinyML devices to large language models has made energy efficiency a primary constraint for sustainable AI. Across these scales, performance and energy are increasingly limited by data movement and memory-system behavior rather than by arithmetic throughput alone. This work reviews energy efficient software hardware codesign methods spanning edge inference and training to datacenter-scale LLM serving, covering accelerator architectures (e.g., ASIC/FPGA dataflows, processing-/compute-in-memory designs) and system-level techniques (e.g., partitioning, quantization, scheduling, and runtime adaptation). We distill common design levers and trade-offs, and highlight recurring gaps including limited cross-platform generalization, large and costly co-design search spaces, and inconsistent benchmarking across workloads and deployment settings. Finally, we outline a hierarchical decomposition perspective that maps optimization strategies to computational roles and supports incremental adaptation, offering practical guidance for building energy and carbon aware ML systems.

### [2] [PowerFlow-DNN: Compiler-Directed Fine-Grained Power Orchestration for End-to-End Edge AI Inference](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.23882)
*Paul Chen,Jeongeun Kim,Wenbo Zhu,Yuanhan Li,Shunyao Huang,Chenjie Weng,Christopher Torng*

Main category: cs.AR

TL;DR: PowerFlow-DNN：一个面向超低功耗DNN加速器的编译器导向框架，通过端到端层间功率调度优化，在截止时间约束下实现能量最小化，相比无功率协调基线可降低37%能耗。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 边缘AI系统面临严格的能量和体积约束，需要极致效率。现有细粒度功率协调技术（如DVFS和电源门控）虽能带来显著能效提升，但面临未解决的挑战：层级方法因层间功率控制决策耦合而产生意外开销，且在实际约束下联合管理这些机制导致组合调度空间爆炸式增长。

Method: 提出PowerFlow-DNN框架，将截止时间约束的实时周期性推理问题形式化为统一的层间功率调度问题。通过构建结构化分层状态图实现高效优化，支持自动化发现能量最小化的功率状态调度方案，同时考虑端到端层间影响。

Result: 在TSMC 40nm工艺的DNN加速器VLSI实现上评估，PowerFlow-DNN在离散化公式下发现接近最优解，能量消耗仅比精确ILP预言机高0.68%，相比无功率协调的激进基线降低能耗达37%。框架能处理超过10^160种可能功率状态分配的组合调度空间，通过轻量剪枝实现2.14倍求解器加速。

Conclusion: PowerFlow-DNN通过编译器导向的端到端功率状态协调，有效解决了边缘AI加速器中的能量效率优化问题，在严格约束下实现了显著的能耗降低，为超低功耗DNN推理提供了可行的解决方案。

Abstract: Edge AI systems often operate under stringent energy and volume constraints that demand extreme efficiency under limited battery capacity, with requirements worsening as intelligent capability demands advance. Prior literature suggests that fine-grained power orchestration, including DVFS and power gating, enables significant energy efficiency benefits that cannot be left unexploited, while still exhibiting unexplored challenges. We observe that layer-level approaches incur unintended overheads due to inter-layer coupling of power control decisions, and that jointly managing these mechanisms under practical constraints such as limited voltage rails and transition overheads leads to a rapidly growing combinatorial schedule space. To address this, we propose PowerFlow-DNN, a compiler-directed framework for end-to-end power-state orchestration in ultra-low-power accelerators. By constructing a rigorous problem formulation for deadline-constrained, real-time, periodic inference as a unified inter-layer power-scheduling problem, our framework enables automated discovery of energy-minimal power-state schedules that adhere to a deadline while accounting for end-to-end, inter-layer impacts. We evaluate the framework on a DNN accelerator VLSI implementation in TSMC 40nm technology. Across representative edge networks, we show that PowerFlow-DNN discovers near-optimal solutions under the discretized formulation and achieves energy within 0.68\% of the exact ILP oracle, reducing energy by up to 37\% compared to an aggressive baseline without power orchestration, while reasoning over a combinatorial schedule space of over $10^{160}$ possible power-state assignments, yet operating on a structured layered state graph that enables efficient optimization, achieving up to 2.14$\times$ solver speedup via lightweight pruning.
