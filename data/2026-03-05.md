### [1] [ARMOR: Robust and Efficient CNN-Based SAR ATR through Model-Hardware Co-Design](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.03598)
*Sachini Wickramasinghe,Tian Ye,Cauligi Raghavendra,Viktor Prasanna*

Main category: cs.AR

TL;DR: 提出一个模型-硬件协同设计框架，将对抗鲁棒性CNN模型压缩与FPGA加速器设计相结合，用于资源受限平台上的SAR自动目标识别。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: CNN在SAR ATR中达到最先进精度，但其高计算成本、延迟和内存占用使其难以部署在资源受限平台（如小型卫星）上。对抗鲁棒性对实际SAR ATR至关重要，但在系统级优化中常被忽视。需要统一框架同时考虑对抗训练模型和硬件约束。

Method: 提出模型-硬件协同设计框架：1) 硬件引导的结构化剪枝，使用FPGA设计导出的硬件性能模型预测剪枝对延迟和资源使用的影响；2) 设计具有通道感知处理单元分配的FPGA加速器，支持全流水线流式架构和时域资源复用架构；3) 自动化设计生成流程将压缩模型映射到优化的FPGA实现。

Result: 在MSTAR和FUSAR-Ship数据集上测试三种CNN架构，框架生成的模型缩小18.3倍，MAC操作减少3.1倍，同时保持鲁棒性。FPGA实现相比CPU基线延迟降低68.1倍，能效提高169.7倍；相比GPU基线延迟降低6.4倍，能效提高33.2倍。

Conclusion: 提出的协同设计框架有效实现了FPGA平台上鲁棒且高效的SAR ATR，平衡了对抗鲁棒性和硬件效率，适用于资源受限部署场景。

Abstract: Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) have achieved state-of-the-art accuracy in Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) Automatic Target Recognition (ATR). However, their high computational cost, latency, and memory footprint make its deployment challenging on resource-constrained platforms such as small satellites. While adversarial robustness is critical for real-world SAR ATR, it is often overlooked in system-level optimizations. Achieving both robustness and inference efficiency requires a unified framework that considers adversarially trained models together with hardware constraints. We present a model-hardware co-design framework for CNN-based SAR ATR that integrates robustness-preserving model compression with FPGA accelerator design. The compression stage includes hardware-guided structured pruning, where a hardware performance model derived from the FPGA design predicts the pruning impact on latency and resource usage. This enables the generation of Pareto-optimal models that improve hardware efficiency under user-defined objectives, while maintaining adversarial robustness within a predefined tolerance. We design an FPGA accelerator with channel-aware Processing Element (PE) allocation that supports both fully pipelined streaming and temporal resource-reuse architectures. An automated design generation flow efficiently maps the compressed models to optimized FPGA implementations. Experiments on the widely used MSTAR and FUSAR-Ship datasets across three CNN architectures show that our framework produces models up to 18.3x smaller with 3.1x fewer MACs while preserving robustness. Our FPGA implementation achieves up to 68.1x (6.4x) lower inference latency and up to 169.7x (33.2x) better energy efficiency compared to CPU (GPU) baselines, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed co-design framework for robust and efficient SAR ATR on FPGA platforms.

### [2] [CarbonPATH: Carbon-aware pathfinding and architecture optimization for chiplet-based AI systems](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.03878)
*Chetan Choppali Sudarshan,Jiajun Hu,Aman Arora,Vidya A. Chhabria*

Main category: cs.AR

TL;DR: CarbonPATH是一个早期路径探索框架，用于在异构集成系统中进行多目标优化，平衡性能、成本和碳足迹，将可持续性作为首要设计约束。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: AI计算需求指数增长导致计算资源需求激增，芯片设计面临极限，同时计算的环境足迹不断扩大。行业向异构集成转型以应对单片扩展的良率和成本挑战，但需要最小化这些复杂系统的碳成本。

Method: CarbonPATH采用协同设计方法，涵盖应用、架构、芯片和封装，通过模拟退火算法探索高维设计空间，优化工作负载映射、架构参数和封装技术。

Result: 该框架能够识别传统方法因限制性假设或有限范围而经常错过的系统级解决方案，平衡传统指标与环境影响。

Conclusion: CarbonPATH为解决异构集成系统中的性能、成本和可持续性多目标挑战提供了有效的早期路径探索框架，将可持续性作为首要设计约束。

Abstract: The exponential growth of AI has created unprecedented demand for computational resources, pushing chip designs to the limit while simultaneously escalating the environmental footprint of computing. As the industry transitions toward heterogeneous integration (HI) to address the yield and cost challenges of monolithic scaling, minimizing the carbon cost of these complex HI systems becomes critical. To fully exploit HI, a co-design approach spanning application, architecture, chip, and packaging is essential. However, this creates a vast design space with competing objectives, specifically the trade-offs between performance, cost, and carbon footprint (CFP) for sustainability. CarbonPATH is an early-stage pathfinding framework designed to address this multi-objective challenge. It identifies optimized HI systems by co-designing workload mapping, architectural parameters, and packaging technologies, while treating sustainability as a first-class design constraint. The framework accounts for a wide range of factors, including compute and memory sizes, chiplet technology nodes, communication protocols, integration style (2D, 2.5D, 3D), operational CFP, embodied CFP, and interconnect type. Using simulated annealing, CarbonPATH explores this high-dimensional space to identify solutions that balance traditional metrics against environmental impact. By capturing interactions across applications, architectures, chiplets, and packaging, CarbonPATH uncovers system-level solutions that traditional methods often miss due to restrictive assumptions or limited scope.

### [3] [Joint Hardware-Workload Co-Optimization for In-Memory Computing Accelerators](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.03880)
*Olga Krestinskaya,Mohammed E. Fouda,Ahmed Eltawil,Khaled N. Salama*

Main category: cs.AR

TL;DR: 提出基于优化进化算法的软硬件协同设计框架，用于设计通用的存内计算加速器架构，通过跨工作负载权衡优化，显著缩小专用设计与通用设计之间的性能差距。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 现有存内计算硬件加速器优化框架大多针对单一工作负载，导致硬件设计高度专业化，无法很好地跨模型和应用泛化。实际部署场景需要单个存内计算平台能高效支持多种神经网络工作负载。

Method: 提出基于优化进化算法的联合硬件-工作负载协同优化框架，通过显式捕获跨工作负载权衡而非针对单一模型优化，设计通用存内计算加速器架构。框架在RRAM和SRAM两种存内计算架构上进行评估。

Result: 优化设计在小型工作负载集（4个）和大型工作负载集（9个）上分别实现高达76.2%和95.5%的能耗-延迟-面积乘积降低。框架展现出强大的鲁棒性和适应性。

Conclusion: 该联合硬件-工作负载协同优化框架能有效设计通用存内计算加速器架构，显著缩小专用设计与通用设计之间的性能差距，为实际部署提供高效的多工作负载支持方案。

Abstract: Software-hardware co-design is essential for optimizing in-memory computing (IMC) hardware accelerators for neural networks. However, most existing optimization frameworks target a single workload, leading to highly specialized hardware designs that do not generalize well across models and applications. In contrast, practical deployment scenarios require a single IMC platform that can efficiently support multiple neural network workloads. This work presents a joint hardware-workload co-optimization framework based on an optimized evolutionary algorithm for designing generalized IMC accelerator architectures. By explicitly capturing cross-workload trade-offs rather than optimizing for a single model, the proposed approach significantly reduces the performance gap between workload-specific and generalized IMC designs. The framework is evaluated on both RRAM- and SRAM-based IMC architectures, demonstrating strong robustness and adaptability across diverse design scenarios. Compared to baseline methods, the optimized designs achieve energy-delay-area product (EDAP) reductions of up to 76.2% and 95.5% when optimizing across a small set (4 workloads) and a large set (9 workloads), respectively. The source code of the framework is available at https://github.com/OlgaKrestinskaya/JointHardwareWorkloadOptimizationIMC.
