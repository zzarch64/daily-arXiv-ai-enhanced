### [1] [AP-DRL: A Synergistic Algorithm-Hardware Framework for Automatic Task Partitioning of Deep Reinforcement Learning on Versal ACAP](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.29369)
*Enlai Li,Zhe Lin,Sharad Sinha,Wei Zhang*

Main category: cs.AR

TL;DR: AP-DRL是一个自动任务划分框架，利用AMD Versal ACAP的异构架构加速深度强化学习训练，通过智能硬件感知优化解决平台选择和量化挑战。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 深度强化学习训练与推理过程紧密耦合，加速训练是重要挑战。现有方法主要针对特定计算单元或优化推理阶段量化，而DRL训练面临两个关键问题：1) 不同算法和操作的计算强度差异大，硬件平台选择复杂；2) 动态范围宽，传统混合精度量化可能导致显著奖励误差。

Method: AP-DRL是一个自动任务划分框架，利用AMD Versal ACAP的异构架构（CPU、FPGA、AI引擎）。首先分析不同DRL工作负载在各组件上的性能瓶颈，然后通过设计空间探索分析和基于整数线性规划的划分模型，将操作匹配到最优计算单元。针对量化挑战，采用硬件感知算法协调FP32（CPU）、FP16（FPGA/DSP）和BF16（AI引擎）操作。

Result: 实验表明，AP-DRL相比可编程逻辑基线可实现最高4.17倍加速，相比AI引擎基线可实现最高3.82倍加速，同时保持训练收敛性。

Conclusion: AP-DRL通过智能硬件感知的任务划分和量化优化，有效解决了DRL训练中的平台选择和精度挑战，在AMD Versal ACAP异构平台上实现了显著的训练加速。

Abstract: Deep reinforcement learning has demonstrated remarkable success across various domains. However, the tight coupling between training and inference processes makes accelerating DRL training an essential challenge for DRL optimization. Two key issues hinder efficient DRL training: (1) the significant variation in computational intensity across different DRL algorithms and even among operations within the same algorithm complicates hardware platform selection, while (2) DRL's wide dynamic range could lead to substantial reward errors with conventional FP16+FP32 mixed-precision quantization. While existing work has primarily focused on accelerating DRL for specific computing units or optimizing inference-stage quantization, we propose AP-DRL to address the above challenges.
  AP-DRL is an automatic task partitioning framework that harnesses the heterogeneous architecture of AMD Versal ACAP (integrating CPUs, FPGAs, and AI Engines) to accelerate DRL training through intelligent hardware-aware optimization. Our approach begins with bottleneck analysis of CPU, FPGA, and AIE performance across diverse DRL workloads, informing the design principles for AP-DRL's inter-component task partitioning and quantization optimization. The framework then addresses the challenge of platform selection through design space exploration-based profiling and ILP-based partitioning models that match operations to optimal computing units based on their computational characteristics. For the quantization challenge, AP-DRL employs a hardware-aware algorithm coordinating FP32 (CPU), FP16 (FPGA/DSP), and BF16 (AI Engine) operations by leveraging Versal ACAP's native support for these precision formats. Comprehensive experiments indicate that AP-DRL can achieve speedup of up to 4.17$\times$ over programmable logic and up to 3.82$\times$ over AI Engine baselines while maintaining training convergence.

### [2] [CXLRAMSim v1.0: System-Level Exploration of CXL Memory Expander Cards](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.29483)
*Karan Pathak,David Atienza,Marina Zapater*

Main category: cs.AR

TL;DR: 提出了CXLRAMSim，首个集成到gem5中的全系统模拟器，能够准确模拟CXL设备在I/O总线上的正确位置，支持未修改的Linux内核和软件栈，提供真实的延迟带宽行为和与系统DRAM的真正交错访问。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 随着大语言模型训练和推理需求的增长，基于CXL的加载/存储互连的扩展系统加速采用。现有模拟工具依赖简化或不兼容的架构模型，影响准确性和可用性，需要更精确的全系统模拟。

Method: 开发了CXLRAMSim，集成到gem5模拟器中，将CXL设备建模在I/O总线的正确位置，支持未修改的Linux内核和软件栈，实现真实的延迟带宽行为和与系统DRAM的真正交错访问。

Result: 提供了高保真的CXL.mem特性描述，能够捕捉关键挑战，如访问CXL内存时的缓存污染问题。

Conclusion: CXLRAMSim解决了现有CXL模拟工具的局限性，为准确模拟基于CXL的扩展系统架构提供了有效工具，有助于研究和优化此类系统的性能。

Abstract: The growing demands in the training and inference of Large Language Models (LLMs) are accelerating the adoption of scale-up systems that extend server shared memory through the use of Compute Express Link (CXL)-based load/store interconnects. Accurate full-system simulation of such architectures remains challenging, as existing tools (all very recent) rely on simplified or non-compliant architectural models, impacting accuracy and usability. We present CXLRAMSim, the first gem5-integrated, full-system simulator that models CXL devices at their correct position on the I/O bus, enabling the use of unmodified Linux kernels and software stack, realistic latency-bandwidth behavior and true interleaving with system DRAM. Our approach provides high-fidelity CXL.mem characterization and captures key challenges such as cache pollution when accessing CXL memory.

### [3] [HLC: A High-Quality Lightweight Mezzanine Codec Featuring High-Throughput Palette](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.29864)
*Chenlong He,Leilei Huang,Wei Li,Hanyang Cui,Zhijian Hao,Xiaoyang Zeng,Yibo Fan*

Main category: cs.AR

TL;DR: HLC是一种针对屏幕内容优化的高质量轻量级编解码器，通过无数据依赖的调色板技术解决现有中间编解码器在文本密集区域的质量问题，在保持高吞吐量的同时显著提升压缩性能。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 现有中间图像编解码器缺乏专门的屏幕内容编码工具，在带宽限制下难以保持高质量，特别是在文本密集区域。虽然分发编解码器提供先进的屏幕内容压缩技术，但其高计算复杂度使其不适用于中间编码。

Method: 提出高质量轻量级编解码器(HLC)，核心创新是无数据依赖的调色板技术，消除吞吐量瓶颈。同时设计了码率-失真优化模块在调色板和传统预测模式之间进行仲裁，以及在码率估计和熵编码之间采用数据重用策略以最小化硬件资源需求。

Result: 与4K@120fps JPEG-XS编码器相比，HLC在相同吞吐量下仅使用一半的LUT资源，并在游戏、自然和文本内容数据集上分别实现3.461dB、3.299dB和5.312dB的BD-PSNR改进。

Conclusion: HLC成功解决了中间编解码器在屏幕内容编码方面的不足，通过创新的无数据依赖调色板技术实现了高质量、高吞吐量且硬件资源高效的屏幕内容压缩方案。

Abstract: Existing mezzanine image codecs lack specialized screen content coding tools and therefore struggle to maintain high image quality under bandwidth constraints, especially in areas with dense text. Although distribution codecs offer advanced screen content compression techniques, their high computational complexity makes them impractical for mezzanine coding. To address this shortfall, we introduce the High-quality Lightweight Codec (HLC), a solution centered on enabling practical, high-throughput palette for mezzanine coding. The core innovation is a novel data-dependency-free palette that eliminates the throughput bottlenecks. To ensure its effectiveness across all content, a co-designed rate-distortion optimization module arbitrates between the palette and traditional prediction modes, while a data reuse strategy between rate estimation and entropy coding minimizes the overall hardware resources required for the system. Experimental results show that, compared with a 4K@120fps JPEG-XS encoder, HLC achieves the same throughput while using only half the LUT resources and delivers BD-PSNR improvements of 3.461dB, 3.299dB, and 5.312dB on gaming, natural, and text content datasets, respectively.

### [4] [SISA: A Scale-In Systolic Array for GEMM Acceleration](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.29913)
*Luigi Altamura,Alessio Cicero,Mateo Vázquez Maceiras,Mohammad Ali Maleki,Pedro Trancoso*

Main category: cs.AR

TL;DR: SISA是一种新型脉动阵列架构，通过将传统方形阵列分割为水平矩形板，解决了LLM中矩阵形状不规则导致的硬件利用率低的问题。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 传统基于方形脉动阵列的矩阵加速器在处理LLM时面临挑战，因为LLM的矩阵形状具有输入依赖性和高度偏斜性，导致硬件资源利用率低下。

Method: 提出SISA架构，将传统方形脉动阵列分割为水平矩形板，通过独立调度各板来高效执行小型或偏斜矩阵，同时保留完整阵列操作处理大型GEMM。

Result: 与相同PE数量的最先进单片脉动阵列相比，SISA在代表性LLM上实现了最高8.52倍加速和93%的能量延迟积降低。

Conclusion: SISA架构通过创新的脉动阵列分割设计，有效解决了LLM中矩阵形状不规则导致的硬件利用率问题，显著提升了性能和能效。

Abstract: The currently dominant AI/ML workloads, such as Large Language Models (LLMs), rely on the efficient execution of General Matrix-Matrix Multiplication (GEMM) operations. Thus, most systems are equipped with dedicated matrix hardware accelerators based on square Systolic Arrays (SAs) of Processing Elements (PEs). While this organization was effective for traditional Deep Neural Networks (DNNs), LLMs introduce input-dependent and highly skewed matrices, leading to underutilized SA resources. To address this challenge, we propose SISA (Scale-In Systolic Array), a novel SA architecture that partitions the traditional square array into horizontal rectangular slabs. With minimal overhead, SISA exposes parallelism through independently scheduled slabs for efficient execution of small or skewed matrix shapes, while retaining full-array operation for large GEMMs. SISA achieves up to 8.52x speedup and 93% energy-delay-product (EDP) reduction for representative LLMs compared to a state-of-the-art monolithic SA with the same number of PEs.
