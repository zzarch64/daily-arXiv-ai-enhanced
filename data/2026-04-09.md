### [1] [CoverAssert: Iterative LLM Assertion Generation Driven by Functional Coverage via Syntax-Semantic Representations](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.06607)
*Yonghao Wang,Yang Yin,Hongqin Lyu,Jiaxin Zhou,Zhiteng Chao,Mingyu Shi,Wenchao Ding,Yunlin Du,Jing Ye,Tiancheng Wang,Huawei Li*

Main category: cs.AR

TL;DR: CoverAssert是一个迭代框架，通过聚类断言特征、映射到规范，并利用功能覆盖率反馈指导LLM优先处理未覆盖点，显著提升SystemVerilog断言生成的质量和覆盖率。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: LLM虽然能从自然语言规范生成SystemVerilog断言，但单次生成的断言往往缺乏功能覆盖率，因为LLM对IC设计的理解有限。现有方法无法有效确保生成的断言能充分覆盖设计功能。

Method: 提出CoverAssert迭代框架：1) 聚类断言的语义和AST结构特征；2) 将断言映射到规范要求；3) 利用功能覆盖率反馈指导LLM优先处理未覆盖的功能点，通过迭代优化提升覆盖率。

Result: 在四个开源设计上的实验表明，CoverAssert与AssertLLM和Spec2Assertion集成后，平均分支覆盖率提升9.57%，语句覆盖率提升9.64%，翻转覆盖率提升15.69%。

Conclusion: CoverAssert通过迭代优化和覆盖率反馈机制，有效解决了LLM生成SystemVerilog断言时覆盖率不足的问题，显著提升了断言的质量和设计验证效果。

Abstract: LLMs can generate SystemVerilog assertions (SVAs) from natural language specs, but single-pass outputs often lack functional coverage due to limited IC design understanding. We propose CoverAssert, an iterative framework that clusters semantic and AST-based structural features of assertions, maps them to specifications, and uses functional coverage feedback to guide LLMs in prioritizing uncovered points. Experiments on four open-source designs show that integrating CoverAssert with AssertLLM and Spec2Assertion improves average improvements of 9.57 % in branch coverage, 9.64 % in statement coverage, and 15.69 % in toggle coverage.

### [2] [SwarmIO: Towards 100 Million IOPS SSD Emulation for Next-generation GPU-centric Storage Systems](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.06668)
*Hyeseong Kim,Gwangoo Yeo,Minsoo Rhu*

Main category: cs.AR

TL;DR: SwarmIO是一个面向GPU中心存储的SSD模拟器，能够模拟高达4000万IOPS的IOPS优化SSD，相比现有基线模拟器在GPU启动I/O下实现303.9倍加速。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: GPU启动I/O和超高随机读取IOPS SSD的发展推动了GPU中心存储系统的出现，但现有框架无法对这种系统进行定量端到端评估。传统SSD模拟器在GPU中心设置下面临三个关键挑战：前端可扩展性有限、GPU启动I/O控制路径软件开销高、以及极高I/O请求率下的时序模型维护开销过大。

Method: 提出SwarmIO，一个面向大规模并行GPU中心存储的SSD模拟器。它能够精确模拟IOPS优化的SSD，支持高达4000万IOPS的性能目标，解决了传统模拟器在GPU启动I/O环境下的可扩展性、软件开销和时序模型维护问题。

Result: SwarmIO在GPU启动I/O下相比最先进的基线SSD模拟器实现了303.9倍的加速。通过向量搜索案例研究显示，将SSD IOPS从250万提升到4000万，平均端到端加速可达9.7倍。

Conclusion: SwarmIO为GPU中心存储系统提供了一个有效的端到端评估框架，能够精确模拟高性能IOPS优化SSD，解决了传统模拟器在GPU启动I/O环境下的关键限制，为未来GPU中心存储系统设计和优化提供了重要工具。

Abstract: GPU-initiated I/O has emerged as a key mechanism for achieving high-throughput storage access by leveraging massive GPU thread-level parallelism, while recent industry trends point toward SSDs optimized for ultra-high random-read IOPS. Together, these trends are enabling the emergence of IOPS-optimized, GPU-centric storage systems. Despite this momentum, no existing framework enables quantitative end-to-end evaluation of storage systems optimized for GPU-initiated I/O. While conventional SSD emulators provide a promising path toward end-to-end modeling in traditional storage systems, they face three key challenges in this GPU-centric setting: limited frontend scalability for ingesting massive request streams, high software overhead in emulating GPU-initiated I/O control and data paths, and excessive timing-model maintenance overhead at extremely high I/O request rates. We propose SwarmIO, an SSD emulator for massively parallel, GPU-centric storage. SwarmIO faithfully models IOPS-optimized SSDs at target performance levels of up to 40 MIOPS, achieving a 303.9x speedup over the state-of-the-art baseline SSD emulator under GPU-initiated I/O. We further demonstrate its utility through a vector search case study, showing that increasing SSD IOPS from 2.5 MIOPS to 40 MIOPS yields an average end-to-end speedup of up to 9.7x.

### [3] [CBM-Dual: A 65-nm Fully Connected Chaotic Boltzmann Machine Processor for Dual Function Simulated Annealing and Reservoir Computing](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.06808)

Main category: cs.AR

TL;DR: 首个硅验证的数字混沌动力学处理器CBM-Dual，支持模拟退火和储层计算，用于边缘AI实时决策和轻量级适应。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 为自主边缘AI提供实时决策和轻量级适应能力，解决数字混沌动力学处理器的高计算和面积成本问题。

Method: 1) 利用神经元翻转率低的特性设计CBM专用调度器，减少99%乘累加操作；2) 采用高效乘法分割方案，减少59%面积；3) 实现1024神经元全连接混沌玻尔兹曼机。

Result: 在65nm工艺下实现12mm²芯片，支持异构任务并行执行，在模拟退火和储层计算领域分别实现25-54倍和4.5倍的能效提升。

Conclusion: CBM-Dual是首个硅验证的双模数字混沌处理器，通过创新调度和架构优化显著提升能效，为边缘AI提供高效计算平台。

Abstract: This paper presents CBM-Dual, the first silicon-proven digital chaotic dynamics processor (CDP) supporting both simulated annealing (SA) and reservoir computing (RC). CBM-Dual enables real-time decision-making and lightweight adaptation for autonomous Edge AI, employing the largest-scale fully connected 1024-neuron chaotic Boltzmann machine (CBM). To address the high computational and area costs of digital CDPs, we propose: 1) a CBM-specific scheduler that exploits an inherently low neuron flip rate to reduce multiply-accumulate operations by 99%, and 2) an efficient multiply splitting scheme that reduces the area by 59%. Fabricated in 65nm (12mm$^2$), CBM-Dual achieves simultaneous heterogeneous task execution and state-of-the-art energy efficiency, delivering $\times$25-54 and $\times$4.5 improvements in the SA and RC fields, respectively.

### [4] [TRAPTI: Time-Resolved Analysis for SRAM Banking and Power Gating Optimization in Embedded Transformer Inference](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.06955)
*Jan Klhufek,Alberto Marchisio,Vojtech Mrazek,Lukas Sekanina,Muhammad Shafique*

Main category: cs.AR

TL;DR: TRAPTI是一个两阶段框架，通过周期级推理仿真和时间分辨的内存占用分析来优化Transformer在嵌入式硬件上的KV缓存管理，对比了GPT-2 XL和DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-1.5B的内存配置。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: Transformer在嵌入式硬件部署时面临面积、延迟和能耗限制，其中KV缓存的内存占用随序列长度增长，严重制约片上内存利用率。现有GQA机制虽能减少KV缓存需求，但需要理解片上内存需求如何随时间演变才能有效利用这些优化。

Method: 提出TRAPTI两阶段方法：第一阶段通过周期级推理仿真获取内存占用轨迹和访问统计；第二阶段利用这些轨迹探索分块内存组织和电源门控配置的离线优化流程。应用于GPT-2 XL和DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-1.5B进行MHA和GQA内存配置对比。

Result: 分析显示，在相同加速器配置下，DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-1.5B相比GPT-2 XL实现了2.72倍的峰值片上内存利用率降低，为电源门控优化提供了更多机会。

Conclusion: TRAPTI框架通过时间分辨的内存分析为Transformer在嵌入式硬件上的部署提供了设计指导，证明了GQA相比MHA在内存效率上的显著优势，并为电源门控等优化创造了条件。

Abstract: Transformer neural networks achieve state-of-the-art accuracy across language and vision tasks, but their deployment on embedded hardware is hindered by stringent area, latency, and energy constraints. During inference, performance and efficiency are increasingly dominated by the Key--Value (KV) cache, whose memory footprint grows with sequence length, straining on-chip memory utilization. Although existing mechanisms such as Grouped-Query Attention (GQA) reduce KV cache requirements compared to Multi-Head Attention (MHA), effectively exploiting this reduction requires understanding how on-chip memory demand evolves over time. This work presents TRAPTI, a two-stage methodology that combines cycle-level inference simulation with time-resolved analysis of on-chip memory occupancy to guide design decisions. In the first stage, the framework obtains memory occupancy traces and memory access statistics from simulation. In the second stage, the framework leverages the traces to explore banked memory organizations and power-gating configurations in an offline optimization flow. We apply this methodology to GPT-2 XL and DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-1.5B under the same accelerator configuration, enabling a direct comparison of MHA and GQA memory profiles. The analysis shows that DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-1.5B exhibits a 2.72x reduction in peak on-chip memory utilization in this setting compared to GPT-2 XL, unlocking further opportunities for power-gating optimization.

</details>


### [5] [Symbolic Polyhedral-Based Energy Analysis for Nested Loop Programs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07287)
*Avinash Mahesh Nirmala,Dominik Walter,Frank Hannig,Jürgen Teich*

Main category: cs.AR

TL;DR: 提出一种符号化方法，用于估计嵌套循环程序在并行处理器阵列加速器架构上映射和调度时的能耗，替代基于仿真的评估。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 传统基于仿真的能耗评估方法无法扩展，无法有效支持设计空间探索。需要一种能够捕捉映射和调度决策对处理器阵列能耗影响的符号化分析方法。

Method: 开发符号化能耗分析方法，分析循环嵌套在处理器阵列上的映射和调度决策对能耗的影响。该方法不依赖于问题规模，与基于仿真的方法形成对比。

Result: 与基于仿真的结果相比，符号化分析不受问题规模限制，具有可扩展性。该方法适用于设计空间探索、阵列规模变化影响研究以及与其他循环嵌套加速器架构的比较。

Conclusion: 提出的符号化能耗分析方法为处理器阵列加速器的映射和调度决策提供了有效的设计空间探索工具，解决了传统仿真方法无法扩展的问题。

Abstract: This work presents a symbolic approach for estimating the energy consumption for nested loop programs when mapped and scheduled on parallel processor array accelerator architectures. Instead of simulation-based evaluation, we derive a methodology for symbolic energy analysis that captures the impact of mapping and scheduling decisions of loop nests on processor arrays. We compare our approach against simulation-based results for selected benchmarks and varying sizes of the iteration spaces. Whereas the latter are not scalable, our symbolic analysis is shown to be independent of the problem size. The presented evaluation methodology can be beneficially used during the design space exploration of mapping and scheduling decisions, for studying the influence of array size variations, and for comparisons with other loop nest accelerator architectures.
