Abstract: On-edge machine learning (ML) often strives to maximize the intelligence of small models while miniaturizing the circuit size and power needed to perform inference. Despite these benefits, the trade-offs between LGN parameters and resulting hardware synthesis characteristics are not well characterized. ### [3] [RangeGuard: Efficient, Bounded Approximate Error Correction for Reliable DNNs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.04563)
*Hanum Ko,Sangheum Yeon,Jong Hwan Ko,Jungrae Kim*

Main category: cs.AR

TL;DR: RangeGuard是一种面向DNN/LLM的元数据纠错框架，通过压缩范围标识符（RID）在仅用16位奇偶校验位的情况下，可容忍64+位翻转错误，保证推理精度不受影响。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 随着DRAM密度增加和3D集成发展，原始故障率上升，多比特错误不再罕见。DNN虽容忍小数值扰动，但随机比特翻转会产生极端异常值并传播，严重降低精度。LLM由于注意力、残差和归一化层会放大并保持单个污染激活值，尤其脆弱。

Method: 提出元数据中心的纠错框架RangeGuard，基于有界近似校正。编码紧凑的范围标识符（RID）捕获每个值的数值范围，将有限的冗余集中于范围变化（表示有害语义偏差），忽略良性范围内变化。检测到范围变化时，恢复正确范围并替换代表值，确保错误幅度被限制在范围内。

Result: 基于RID，RangeGuard仅用GPU内存中可用的16位奇偶校验位，即可容忍64+比特翻转，且无显著的精度损失。

Conclusion: 通过引入语义范围保护，RangeGuard即使在频繁内存错误和严格冗余预算下也能实现可靠的DNN执行。

Abstract: As DRAM scales in density and adopts 3D integration, raw fault rates increase and multi-bit errors are no longer rare. Such errors can severely impact Deep Neural Networks (DNNs): although DNNs tolerate small numerical perturbations, random bit flips can create extreme outliers that propagate and sharply degrade accuracy. Large Language Models (LLMs) are particularly vulnerable because attention, residual, and normalization layers can amplify and preserve a single corrupted activation across many layers, destabilizing inference.
  This paper introduces RangeGuard, a metadata-centric error-correcting framework that provides strong reliability and high efficiency based on bounded approximate correction. Instead of protecting raw bits, RangeGuard encodes compact Range Identifiers (RIDs) that capture the numerical range of each value. These compact metadata enable efficient use of limited redundancy and concentrate protection on range changes, which indicate harmful semantic deviations, while ignoring benign intra-range variations. Upon detecting a range change, RangeGuard restores the correct range and substitutes a representative value, ensuring that error magnitudes are bounded within the range. Based on RIDs, RangeGuard can tolerate 64+ flipped bits using only 16 bits of parity available in GPU memories without a noticeable accuracy loss. By introducing semantic range protection, RangeGuard enables reliable DNN execution even under frequent memory errors and tight redundancy budgets.
