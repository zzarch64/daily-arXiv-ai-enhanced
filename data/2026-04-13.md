### [1] [Memory Wall is not gone: A Critical Outlook on Memory Architecture in Digital Neuromorphic Computing](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.08774)
*Amirreza Yousefzadeh,Sameed Sohail,Ana Lucia Varbanescu*

Main category: cs.AR

TL;DR: 数字神经形态处理器虽然旨在解决冯·诺依曼架构的内存墙问题，但片上内存系统（SRAM、STT-MRAM等）已成为面积和能耗的主要消耗者，形成了新的内存墙，可能阻碍其在边缘和嵌入式应用中的竞争力。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 神经形态技术的快速发展旨在解决传统冯·诺依曼架构固有的内存墙挑战。本文旨在批判性地审视当前数字神经形态处理器及其缓解这一瓶颈的策略。

Method: 通过分析各种内存技术（包括SRAM和新兴技术如STT-MRAM）的能耗和面积效率，评估数字神经形态处理器中的片上内存系统。

Result: 研究发现，虽然数字神经形态处理器通过分布式架构将计算靠近内存，但片上内存系统已成为面积和能耗的主要消耗者，导致了新的内存墙问题。

Conclusion: 如果不重新评估内存组织，数字神经形态处理器可能难以在边缘和嵌入式应用中有效竞争。文章最后提出了克服神经形态系统中片上内存限制的未来研究方向。

Abstract: The rapid advancement of neuromorphic technology aims to address the memory wall challenge inherent in conventional von Neumann architectures. This paper critically examines current digital neuromorphic processors and their strategies to mitigate this bottleneck. While designed to bring computation closer to memory through distributed architectures, our findings indicate that on-chip memory systems, including SRAM and emerging technologies like STT-MRAM, have become significant consumers of area and energy, leading to a new memory wall. Through an analysis of energy and area efficiency in various memory technologies, we argue that without a re-evaluation of memory organization, digital neuromorphic processors may struggle to compete effectively in edge and embedded applications. We conclude with potential pathways for future research to overcome the limitations of on-chip memory in neuromorphic systems.

### [2] [From Indiscriminate to Targeted: Efficient RTL Verification via Functionally Key Signal-Driven LLM Assertion Generation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.08932)
*Yonghao Wang,Hongqin Lyu,Boling Chen,MinYang Bao,Wenchao Ding,Feng Gu,Zhiteng Chao,Jianan Mu,Kan Shi,Tiancheng Wang,Huawei Li*

Main category: cs.AR

TL;DR: AgileAssert是一个基于关键信号驱动的断言生成框架，通过RTL语义图识别关键信号，引导LLM生成针对性断言，显著减少断言数量同时提升覆盖率。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 现有基于LLM的断言生成方法追求无差别的全面验证，而工业实践需要在最小验证成本下最大化覆盖率。如何识别对设计功能性和错误传播影响最大的关键信号，实现从无差别到针对性验证的转变，是当前的主要挑战。

Method: 1. 构建RTL语义图；2. 通过混合评分和选择机制识别top-K关键信号；3. 结构感知的RTL切片为LLM提供精确目标和上下文信息；4. 引导LLM生成紧密约束的针对性断言。

Result: 在模块级和CPU级设计上评估：平均减少66.68%的断言数量，优于三种现有SOTA方法，显著提升覆盖率指标，同时减少64%的输入token消耗。在突变测试中，当错误检测率超过现有方法时，平均使用的断言数量减少72.74%。

Conclusion: AgileAssert通过关键信号驱动的断言生成，实现了从无差别验证到针对性验证的转变，在显著减少断言数量的同时提升验证效率，为工业实践提供了有效的解决方案。

Abstract: Functional verification has become the most time-consuming phase in IC development, and Assertion-Based Verification (ABV) is key to reducing debugging time. However, existing LLM-based assertion generation methods typically pursue indiscriminate verification, aiming for maximal coverage without considering signal criticality, whereas industrial practice demands maximizing coverage with minimal verification cost. Consequently, identifying signals that have the greatest impact on design functionality and error propagation-enabling a shift from indiscriminate to targeted verification-remains a key challenge. To address this, we propose AgileAssert, a key signal-driven assertion generation framework that constructs RTL semantic graphs and identifies the top-K critical signals via a hybrid scoring and selection mechanism, followed by structure-aware RTL slicing to provide the LLM with precise targets and contextual information, thereby guiding LLMs to generate tightly constrained targeted assertions for efficient verification. Evaluated on block- and CPU-level designs, with an average 66.68% reduction in assertions, our approach outperforms three existing SOTA methods, and significantly improving coverage metrics while reducing input token consumption by 64%. In mutation testing, when our approach surpasses existing methods in error detection rate, the average number of assertions used decreases by 72.74%.

### [3] [DRIFT: Harnessing Inherent Fault Tolerance for Efficient and Reliable Diffusion Model Inference](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.09073)
*Jinqi Wen,Tong Xie,Runsheng Wang,Meng Li*

Main category: cs.AR

TL;DR: DRIFT：一种算法-架构协同优化框架，利用扩散模型的容错性实现高效可靠的推理，通过细粒度DVFS策略和回滚ABFT机制，在保持生成质量的同时实现36%能耗节省或1.7倍加速


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 扩散模型部署面临高能耗和推理延迟问题，现有DVFS方法要么效率提升有限，要么输出质量下降，因为它们忽略了扩散模型固有的容错特性

Method: 1）对代表性扩散模型进行全面容错性分析；2）提出细粒度、容错感知的DVFS策略，选择性保护错误敏感的网络块和时间步；3）基于回滚算法的容错机制，自适应纠正关键错误；4）优化卸载间隔和重组数据布局以减少内存开销

Result: 在多种模型和数据集上的实验表明，DRIFT通过电压降频可实现平均36%的能耗节省，或通过超频实现1.7倍加速，同时保持生成质量

Conclusion: DRIFT框架成功利用扩散模型的容错特性，通过算法-架构协同优化实现了高效可靠的推理，为扩散模型部署提供了有效的能效优化解决方案

Abstract: Diffusion model deployment has been suffering from high energy consumption and inference latency despite its superior performance in visual generation tasks. Dynamic voltage and frequency scaling (DVFS) offers a promising solution to exploit the potential of the underlying accelerators. However, existing approaches often lead to either limited efficiency gains or degraded output quality because they overlook the inherent fault tolerance of the diffusion model. Therefore, in this paper, we propose DRIFT, a novel algorithmarchitecture co-optimization framework that harnesses the fault tolerance for efficient and reliable diffusion model inference. We first perform a comprehensive resilience analysis on representative diffusion models. Building on these observations, we introduce a fine-grained, resilience-aware DVFS strategy that selectively protects error-sensitive network blocks and timesteps, and a rollback algorithm-based fault tolerance (ABFT) mechanism that adaptively corrects only critical errors by reverting to previous timesteps. We further optimize offloading intervals and reorganize data layouts to reduce memory overhead. Experiments across diverse models and datasets show that DRIFT can achieve on average 36% energy savings through voltage underscaling or 1.7x speedup via overclocking while maintaining generation quality.

### [4] [A 0.5-V Linear Neuromorphic Voltage-to-Spike Encoder Using a Bulk-Driven Transconductor](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.09315)
*Meysam Akbari,Erika Covi,Kea-Tiong Tang*

Main category: cs.AR

TL;DR: 提出一种超低功耗电压-脉冲编码器，通过线性化体驱动跨导与DPI LIF神经元结合，实现近线性电压-脉冲频率转换，在0.5V供电下功耗仅22-180nW


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 神经形态计算需要高效的电压-脉冲编码器，传统编码器存在非线性问题且功耗较高，需要设计超低功耗、高线性度的编码器用于边缘计算应用

Method: 采用无尾体驱动差分对改善大信号线性度，使用跨导线性化网络抑制主导的sinh非线性，结合DPI前端实现电流-脉冲转换的线性化

Result: 在TSMC 0.18-um CMOS工艺中实现，0.5V供电下，输入0.1-0.4V范围内线性度偏差小于5.6%，功耗22-180nW，面积0.0074mm²

Conclusion: 该电压-脉冲编码器实现了超低功耗下的高线性度转换，为神经形态边缘计算提供了高效的编码解决方案

Abstract: This work introduces an ultralow-power voltage-to-spike encoder that achieves near-linear voltage-to-firing-rate conversion by pairing a linearized bulk-driven transconductor with a DPI-based LIF neuron. A tail-less bulk-driven differential pair improves large-signal linearity, while a translinear linearization network suppresses the dominant sinh nonlinearity and stabilizes the bias-tunable V-to-I gain. The resulting current feeds a DPI front-end that linearizes current-to-spike conversion. Fabricated in TSMC 0.18-um CMOS and operating at VDD = 0.5 V with 2-27 nA reference current, the encoder achieves a deviation of less than 5.6 percent from linearity over 0.1-0.4 V input, consumes 22-180 nW, and occupies 0.0074 mm^2.

</details>
