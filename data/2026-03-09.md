### [1] [LUMINA: LLM-Guided GPU Architecture Exploration via Bottleneck Analysis](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05904)
*Tao Zhang,Rui Ma,Shuotao Xu,Peng Cheng,Yongqiang Xiong*

Main category: cs.AR

TL;DR: LUMINA是一个基于大语言模型的GPU架构探索框架，通过AI提升设计空间探索效率，在470万可能设计中仅用20步找到比A100 GPU性能更好、面积更优的6个设计。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 现代AI工作负载（如LLM推理）的GPU设计空间探索面临三大挑战：巨大的多模态设计空间、高昂的仿真成本、复杂的多目标优化（性能、功耗、面积权衡）。现有自动化方法要么需要过多探索样本，要么依赖人工启发式分析，成本过高。

Method: LUMINA从仿真器代码中提取架构知识，进行敏感性分析自动生成DSE规则，并在探索过程中自动修正。核心组件是DSE Benchmark，全面评估和增强LLM在架构优化所需的三个基本技能，为模型选择和架构推理提供可重现基础。

Result: 在470万可能样本的设计空间中，LUMINA仅用20步就找到6个比A100 GPU性能更好、面积更优的设计。相比机器学习基线，LUMINA实现了17.5倍的探索效率和32.9%更好的设计质量（Pareto Hypervolume）。

Conclusion: LUMINA展示了LLM驱动的GPU架构探索能够以最小搜索成本提供高质量设计指导，显著提升设计空间探索的效率和效果，为复杂硬件架构优化提供了新的AI驱动方法。

Abstract: GPU design space exploration (DSE) for modern AI workloads, such as Large-Language Model (LLM) inference, is challenging because of GPUs' vast, multi-modal design spaces, high simulation costs, and complex design optimization objectives (e.g. performance, power and area trade-offs). Existing automated DSE methods are often prohibitively expensive, either requiring an excessive number of exploration samples or depending on intricate, manually crafted analyses of interdependent critical paths guided by human heuristics.
  We present LUMINA, an LLM-driven GPU architecture exploration framework that leverage AI to enhance the DSE efficiency and efficacy for GPUs. LUMINA extracts architectural knowledge from simulator code and performs sensitivity studies to automatically compose DSE rules,which are auto-corrected during exploration. A core component of LUMINA is a DSE Benchmark that comprehensively evaluates and enhances LLMs' capabilities across three fundamental skills required for architecture optimization, which provides a principled and reproducible basis for model selection and ensuring consistent architectural reasoning.
  In the design space with 4.7 million possible samples, LUMINA identifies 6 designs of better performance and area than an A100 GPU efficiently, using only 20 steps via LLM-assisted bottleneck analysis. In comparison, LUMINA achieves 17.5x higher than design space exploration efficiency, and 32.9% better designs (i.e. Pareto Hypervolume) than Machine-Learning baselines, showcasing its ability to deliver high-quality design guidance with minimal search cost.

### [2] [A Persistent-State Dataflow Accelerator for Memory-Bound Linear Attention Decode on FPGA](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05931)
*Neelesh Gupta,Peter Wang,Rajgopal Kannan,Viktor K. Prasanna*

Main category: cs.AR

TL;DR: GDN是线性注意力机制，用固定大小循环状态替代KV缓存，但GPU上解码时内存受限。本文提出FPGA加速器，将循环状态保持在片上BRAM中，实现4.5倍加速和60倍能效提升。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: GDN等线性注意力机制在解码时存在内存瓶颈，因为每个token都需要从HBM往返读取完整循环状态，导致算术强度低于1 FLOP/B，比标准Transformer更受内存限制。

Method: 设计FPGA加速器，将2MB循环状态持久存储在片上BRAM中，将工作负载从内存受限转为计算受限。采用五级流水线数据路径，每个token对每个状态矩阵只进行一次读写，利用分组值注意力实现配对头并行，并通过数据流流水线重叠准备、计算和输出存储。

Result: 在AMD Alveo U55C上实现，最快配置达到63μs/token，比NVIDIA H100 PCIe GPU快4.5倍。片上功耗9.96W，每token解码能效提升高达60倍。

Conclusion: FPGA加速器通过将循环状态保持在片上BRAM中，有效解决了GDN等线性注意力机制的解码内存瓶颈，显著提升了推理速度和能效。

Abstract: Gated DeltaNet (GDN) is a linear attention mechanism that replaces the growing KV cache with a fixed-size recurrent state. Hybrid LLMs like Qwen3-Next use 75% GDN layers and achieve competitive accuracy to attention-only models. However, at batch-1, GDN decode is memory-bound on GPUs since the full recurrent state must be round-tripped through HBM every token. We show that this bottleneck is architectural, not algorithmic, as all subquadratic sequence models exhibit arithmetic intensities below 1 FLOP/B at decode time, making them more memory-bound than standard Transformers. We present an FPGA accelerator that eliminates this bottleneck by holding the full 2 MB recurrent state persistently in on-chip BRAM, converting the workload from memory-bound to compute-bound. Our design fuses the GDN recurrence into a five-phase pipelined datapath that performs only one read and one write pass over each state matrix per token, exploits Grouped Value Attention for paired-head parallelism, and overlaps preparation, computation, and output storage via dataflow pipelining. We explore four design points on an AMD Alveo U55C using Vitis HLS, varying head-level parallelism from 2 to 16 value-heads per iteration. Our fastest configuration achieves 63 $μ$s per token, 4.5$\times$ faster than the GPU reference on NVIDIA H100 PCIe. Post-implementation power analysis reports 9.96 W on-chip, yielding up to 60$\times$ greater energy efficiency per token decoded.

</details>
