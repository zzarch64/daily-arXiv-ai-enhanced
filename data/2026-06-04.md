### [1] [HighTide: An Agent-Curated Open-Source VLSI Benchmark Suite](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.04126)
*Benjamin Goldblatt,Paolo Pedroso,Farhad Modaresi,Ethan Sifferman,Matthew R. Guthaus*

Main category: cs.AR

TL;DR: HighTide是一个AI辅助的、不断演进的开源基准测试套件，支持多种设计语言和工艺节点，采用Bazel构建增量RTL-to-GDS流程，并集成12种AI智能体技能覆盖全设计生命周期，配有决策日志作为长期记忆，基础设施包含RTL编译验证以确保稳定发布。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 现有基准测试套件缺乏多样性、可扩展性以及AI辅助设计能力，难以满足开源硬件生态系统对不断演进和智能辅助的需求。

Method: 构建了涵盖多种设计语言和工艺节点的开源套件，采用Bazel实现增量RTL-to-GDS编译与远程缓存，设计了12种AI智能体技能覆盖设计生命周期、流程优化、工具参考和元维护，并通过决策日志记录每次调优的 rationale 作为长期记忆，同时搭建了RTL编译验证基础设施确保稳定发布。

Result: 形成了一个公开可用、可随开源硬件生态持续增长的基准测试套件，具备AI辅助设计决策和版本稳定性保障能力。

Conclusion: HighTide通过集成AI智能体和增量编译基础设施，为开源硬件设计提供了一个可持续演进、可复现且具备长期记忆能力的基准测试平台。

Abstract: We introduce HighTide, an evolving AI-assisted benchmark suite. Specifically, the contributions are: (i) a diverse open-source suite spanning multiple design languages and technology nodes, (ii) Bazel-based incremental RTL-to-GDS compilation with remote caching, (iii) AI-assisted design curation through twelve agent skills covering the design lifecycle, flow optimization, tool reference, and meta-maintenance, backed by per-design decision logs that serve as long-term memory of tuning rationale across the suite, and (iv) an infrastructure with RTL compilation verification for stable releases. The suite is publicly available and designed to grow with the open-source hardware ecosystem.

### [2] [GoldenFloat: A Phi-Derived Static-Split Floating-Point Family from GF4 to GF256 with a Lucas-Exact Integer Identity](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05017)
*Dmitrii Vasiliev*

Main category: cs.AR

TL;DR: 提出了一种基于黄金分割比(phi)的静态拆分浮点族GoldenFloat(GF)，包含完整硬件实现和验证工具链，并开放了可证伪的猜想和勘误记录。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 现有浮点数格式（如IEEE 754、posit、takum等）在指数位宽分配上缺乏统一的数学规则，GF试图通过phi^2比例提供一个封闭、一致的指数字段分配规则，覆盖从4位到1024位的多宽度浮点格式。

Method: 对于总宽度N>=4，指数位宽e=round((N-1)/phi^2)，尾数位宽f=N-1-e，其中phi=(1+sqrt(5))/2。提供了开源RTL生成器、Lucas精确累加路径验证和FPGA编解码器实现。

Result: 规则复现了9种现有格式的指数字段宽度，并一致扩展到GF128/512/1024。FPGA编解码器在Artix-7上以323MHz通过35项测试。提供了500位精度的整数累加路径验证。

Conclusion: GF规则在格式覆盖和工具链完整性上有价值，但作者未声称其精度或优越性优于其他格式。通过预注册证伪路径和勘误记录保持开放性和可复现性。

Abstract: We present a hardware-oriented description of GoldenFloat (GF), a static-split floating-point family generated by a single closed rule, and three concrete artefacts: (i) an open multi-width RTL generator covering GF4-GF256 with a continuous-integration differential sweep against a correctly-rounded reference; (ii) an integer-backed Lucas-exact accumulator path verified at 500-digit precision for n = 1, ..., 256; and (iii) a GF16 FPGA codec passing a 35-of-35 testbench at 323 MHz on Artix-7 (Xilinx XC7A35T). For each total width N >= 4, the exponent width is e = round((N-1)/phi^2) with fraction f = N-1-e and phi = (1+sqrt(5))/2. The rule reproduces the realised exponent widths of nine formats (9/9) and extends consistently to GF128, GF512, GF1024. The rule is positioned alongside posit, takum, OCP-MX, and the IEEE P3109 multi-width float draft. We make no per-rung accuracy or superiority claim against any of them. The breadth/toolchain-coherence framing is recorded as an open conjecture with a pre-registered falsification path. A falsification ledger (FL-002) records open questions and the experiments that would settle them. An RTL-correctness erratum dated 2026-05-31 is reported; the fabricated TTSKY26b dies carry the defective multiplier portfolio, and the corrected generator is the regeneration baseline.

</details>
